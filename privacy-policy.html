Privacy Policy

Add-in: Auto Contacts · Last updated: July 2026

Summary: this add-in does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. It has no servers, no accounts, no analytics, and no third-party trackers. Your email and contacts never leave the Microsoft 365 boundary.

What the add-in does

Auto Contacts adds the people you email to your own Outlook contacts. The Add to Contacts button adds the sender of the open message (or, for a message you sent, its recipients). The Contact Sweep pane scans your own sent mail for people who aren't in your contacts yet and lets you add the ones you choose. In both cases, the add-in reads the person's most recent email to you and extracts contact details from their signature — phone, job title, company, website — using simple pattern matching that runs entirely inside your Outlook session. No artificial intelligence or external service is involved.

Data we collect

None. The add-in does not collect, log, or retain any information about you, your email, your contacts, or your usage. Everything it reads and everything it creates stays in your own mailbox.

How your data is handled

When you use the add-in, it signs you in (Microsoft nested app authentication) and uses Microsoft Graph — Microsoft's own Microsoft 365 service — to read your mail and to create or update contacts in your own contacts folder. The data travels only between your mailbox and Microsoft Graph and is processed entirely in your browser/Outlook client. It stays entirely within the Microsoft 365 boundary.

There is no PurposeBuilt Systems server in this process, and no third party receives your data. The add-in connects directly to Microsoft Graph from your own client; we never see, receive, store, or proxy your email or contacts. The add-in accesses only the signed-in user's own mailbox and contacts (delegated permissions Mail.Read and Contacts.ReadWrite) — never anyone else's.

Contacts the add-in creates

Contacts are created only when you click the button or approve them in the sweep checklist, and they are created in your own Outlook contacts, where you can edit or delete them at any time. When a person is already in your contacts, the add-in only fills in blank fields — it never overwrites information you have entered.

Hosting

The add-in's static program files (HTML, JavaScript, icons) are served from GitHub Pages. Serving these files involves no customer data; your email and contact data are never sent to GitHub or to any host.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. Because the add-in collects nothing, we expect it to stay this simple.
