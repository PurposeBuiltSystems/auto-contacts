Add-in: Auto Contacts

What it does

Adds the people you email to your own Outlook contacts — with phone, job title, company, and website pulled automatically from their email signatures. Outlook's autocomplete remembers addresses but never creates real contact cards; Auto Contacts does.

How to use it

  1. Add one person: open a received email and click Add to Contacts on the ribbon. The sender is added to your contacts with details parsed from their signature. (On a message you sent, the recipients are added instead.)
  2. Add everyone you've missed: click Contact Sweep, then Scan my sent mail. The pane lists people you've emailed recently who aren't in your contacts, with a preview of each parsed card. Tick the ones you want and click Add selected.
  3. Sign in / consent the first time (Microsoft prompt). The add-in only accesses your own mailbox and contacts.

Good to know

  • Existing contacts are safe: if a person is already in your contacts, the add-in only fills in blank fields — it never overwrites anything you've entered.
  • Signature parsing is best-effort: a card is created from the person's name and email even when no signature is found; review the details in Outlook People and adjust as needed.
  • Automated senders are skipped in the sweep (no-reply addresses, newsletters, notification bots) — you can disable this in Options.

Troubleshooting

  • Button doesn't appear: make sure you're on a Microsoft 365 / Exchange Online account viewing a message.
  • A field came out wrong: signatures vary wildly; edit the contact in Outlook People — the add-in won't overwrite your correction.
  • Sweep finds nobody: everyone you've emailed in the window is already in your contacts, or your sent folder is empty for that range.

Privacy

Your email and contacts never leave Microsoft 365 — parsing happens in your own Outlook session and we never receive your data. See the Privacy Policy.
