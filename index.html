Auto Contacts

Add the people you email to your Outlook contacts — automatically filled in from their signatures.

One-click add Bulk sweep Signature parsing No data collection Free

Why

Outlook's autocomplete remembers every address you've ever typed — but it never creates an actual contact. Your "contacts" end up living in an unsearchable cache with no phone numbers, no titles, no companies. Auto Contacts fixes that.

What it does

  • Add to Contacts — one click on any email adds the sender to your contacts, with phone, job title, company, and website parsed from their signature.
  • Contact Sweep — scans your sent mail for people you email who aren't in your contacts, previews each card, and adds the ones you pick.
  • Never overwrites — existing contacts only get blank fields filled in.

Privacy by design

No servers, no accounts, no analytics. Everything runs between your Outlook session and Microsoft Graph, inside the Microsoft 365 boundary. Signature parsing is deterministic pattern-matching in your own client — no AI service ever sees your email. Read the privacy policy.
